# Scenario 03 — One-Click All-Hands

**Phase 3 callback.** Shows the meeting primitive: pull the whole
company into one room, interject, end the meeting, watch minutes
and action items generate. Establishes the orchestrator's pause
semantics and the fact that the company has a coherent clock.

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 2 minutes |
| **Exercises** | Phase 3 — M16 (meetings), orchestrator pause/resume |
| **Starts on** | Dashboard → Cards |
| **Ends on** | Dashboard → Cards |

---

## Hook (narrator, 1 line)

> *"Standups, retros, product reviews — one click, with
> agendas, minutes, and action items."*

---

## Setup preconditions

- Scenarios 01 and 02 completed (at least two employees on
  the roster: CEO + Senior Fullstack Engineer).
- One or more open tickets in the Tickets tab (the ticket
  from Scenario 02 is fine).
- Meetings tab visible on the top bar.

---

## Scripted sequence

1. **Navigate to Meetings.**
   - Top-bar tab: **Meetings**.
   - Narrator: *"Empty so far — let's change that."*
2. **Call a meeting.**
   - Target: `data-testid="call-meeting"`.
   - Dialog: **Call Meeting**.
3. **Fill the meeting dialog.**
   - Title: *"Monday all-hands."*
   - Agenda: *"Review open tickets. Align on this week's
     priorities. Surface any blockers."*
   - Attendees: **Select all**.
   - Click **Start**.
4. **Observe the orchestrator pause.**
   - Status indicator in the top bar flips from green dot
     (idle) to amber dot (meeting active).
   - Narrator: *"While the meeting is live the orchestrator
     drains — no new agent work dispatches. The company has
     one clock."*
5. **Watch the first attendee speak.**
   - Meeting detail panel shows a thread. First turn comes
     from the CEO — they read the agenda and open the floor.
   - Narrator: *"Turn-taking is automatic. The orchestrator
     schedules one speaker at a time."*
6. **Interject as Rocky.**
   - Composer at the bottom of the meeting thread.
   - Type: *"Focus on the provider-router ticket first — it's
     blocking documentation."*
   - Send.
7. **End the meeting.**
   - Button: **End Meeting** (top-right of detail panel).
   - Orchestrator resumes. Minutes generate inline.
8. **Show the minutes.**
   - Minutes render as a structured block: *Attendees /
     Key Points / Action Items*. Action items surface as
     candidate tickets.
   - Narrator: *"Action items become tickets with one more
     click — that's the bridge back to Scenario 02's flow."*
9. **Return to Dashboard → Cards.**

---

## Key moments to highlight on camera

- **The amber status dot during the meeting.** Narrator can
  land the "orchestrator is the only scheduler" theme: no
  new tickets, no new agent runs while the meeting is live.
- **The interjection landing in-thread.** The viewer sees
  Rocky participating as a first-class voice, not an observer.
- **Minutes generation.** Minutes are generated by an agent,
  then rendered in a structured panel — not free-form text.

---

## What the viewer just saw

- **One-click meetings with a real pause.** The orchestrator
  drains; nothing else happens; the meeting has the floor.
- **Minutes and action items as first-class output.** The
  meeting is not a chat log — it's a structured event with
  a post-condition.
- **Seamless re-entry into the ticket flow.** Action items
  can be promoted to tickets; the org spins back up.

---

## Dependencies

- Phase 3 M16 — meeting primitive + pause/resume +
  minutes generation.
- Phase 1 M4 — orchestrator.
- Phase 2 M12 — tickets (for action-item promotion).
